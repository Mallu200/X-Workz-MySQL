# What is RDBMS? (Relational Database Management System)

A Relational Database organizes data into **tables** (rows and columns) that are linked together based on data common to each.

### Key Concepts:
* **Table:** A collection of related data (e.g., a "Users" table).
* **Record (Row):** A single entry (e.g., One specific user named "John").
* **Field (Column):** A specific piece of info (e.g., "Email Address").
* **Schema:** The overall design or "blueprint" of the database.

**Why MySQL?** It is open-source, incredibly fast, and used by companies like Facebook, Netflix, and Airbnb to handle billions of rows of data.
